About Indian Navy

The Indian Navy is the maritime branch of the Indian Armed Forces. The President of India is the Supreme Commander of the Indian Navy. The Chief of Naval Staff, a four-star admiral, commands the navy. As a blue-water navy, it operates significantly in the Persian Gulf region, the Horn of Africa, the Strait of Malacca, and routinely conducts anti-piracy operations with other navies in the region. It also conducts routine two to three month-long deployments in the South and East China Seas, as well as in the western Mediterranean Sea simultaneously.

The primary objective of the navy is to safeguard the nation's maritime borders, and in conjunction with other Armed Forces, act to deter or defeat any threats or aggression against the territory, people, or maritime interests of India, both in war and peace. Through joint exercises, goodwill visits, and humanitarian missions including disaster relief, the Indian Navy promotes bilateral relations between nations. Since October 2008, the Indian Navy has maintained at least one frontline warship on continuous deployment in the Gulf of Aden.

As of June 2019, the Indian Navy has over 67,000 active and 75,000 reserve personnel. Its fleet consists of 150 ships and submarines, and over 300 aircraft. As of September 2022, the operational fleet includes 2 aircraft carriers, 1 amphibious transport dock, 4 landing ship tanks, 8 landing craft utility vessels, 14 destroyers, 14 frigates, 2 ballistic missile submarines, 16 diesel-electric attack submarines, 18 corvettes, 1 mine countermeasure vessel, 4 fleet tankers, and various auxiliary vessels and patrol boats. It is considered a blue-water navy capable of regional power projection.

Operations Conducted by Indian Navy

Operation Trident (1971)

A surprise naval attack on Pakistan’s Karachi harbor during the Indo-Pak war. It marked the first time anti-ship missiles were used in combat by the Indian Navy.

Operation Python (1971)

A follow-up naval strike on Karachi, which further damaged Pakistani navy vessels and oil storage facilities after the success of Operation Trident.

Operation Cactus (1988)

India intervened in the Maldives to suppress a coup attempt. The Indian Navy and Air Force played a key role in quickly restoring the legitimate government.

Operation Talwar (1999)

A strategic naval deployment during the Kargil War. The Indian Navy blockaded Pakistani ports and exerted pressure on their maritime supply routes.

Operation Raahat (2015)

The Indian Navy rescued over 4,000 Indians and foreign nationals from Yemen during its civil war, showcasing India's global humanitarian role.

Operation Sankalp (2019–)

Launched to ensure the safe passage of Indian-flagged merchant vessels through the Strait of Hormuz after tensions rose in the region.
