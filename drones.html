About Military Drones

In today's era of modern warfare, technology plays a crucial role in determining the strength and strategy of a nation's military. One such technology that has transformed the battlefield is the unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV), more commonly known as a drone. The Indian Armed Forces have increasingly adopted drones for surveillance, combat, and intelligence purposes. Let's explore the list of drones used by Indian Armed Forces, and take a look at the prominent drones in India's defence arsenal. Drones have become an integral part of modern warfare, providing real-time intelligence, surveillance, and reconnaissance capabilities. The Indian Armed Forces have recognized the significance of drones in enhancing operational efficiency and situational awareness on the battlefield.

Types of Drones

Heron

The Rustom-1 is an Indian unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV) developed by the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). It is designed for surveillance and reconnaissance missions, capable of operating at altitudes of up to 22,000 feet. The Rustom-1 features a wingspan of 20 meters and can carry payloads such as electro-optical sensors and synthetic aperture radar. It has a range of approximately 250 kilometers and can stay airborne for over 12 hours.

Rustom

The Rustom-2, also known as TAPAS (Tactical Aerial Platform for Advanced Surveillance), is an advanced version of the Rustom-1 UAV. It is designed for long-endurance surveillance and reconnaissance missions, with a range of up to 250 kilometers. The Rustom-2 features a wingspan of 20 meters and can carry various payloads, including electro-optical sensors, synthetic aperture radar, and electronic warfare systems.

Netra

The Netra is a small, portable UAV developed by the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) for surveillance and reconnaissance missions. It is designed for quick deployment and can be operated by a small team. The Netra features a wingspan of 1.5 meters and can carry payloads such as electro-optical sensors and infrared cameras. It has a range of approximately 10 kilometers and can stay airborne for over 2 hours.
