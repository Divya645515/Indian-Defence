Rifels

The Indian Army uses a wide range of rifles to meet the diverse needs of its infantry and special forces. The INSAS (Indian Small Arms System) rifle was the standard issue for many years but is now being gradually phased out. It is being replaced by the more advanced SIG716 assault rifles, acquired from the U.S., known for their superior accuracy and reliability. The AK-203, a modernized version of the AK-series rifles, is also being jointly produced in India with Russia and is set to become a major frontline weapon. For close-quarter combat and special operations, elite units use Tavor X95 and AK-47 variants. Sniper rifles like the Dragunov SVD and newer bolt-action rifles are used for long-range engagements. The Army is also focusing on modernizing its small arms with better sights, optics, and modular systems. This evolving arsenal enhances the operational effectiveness of Indian troops in diverse combat environments.

The AK-203 assault

The AK-203 assault rifle is a modern, high-performance firearm developed jointly by India and Russia. It is a derivative of the legendary AK-47, combining its reliability with modern features like improved ergonomics and accuracy. The rifle is chambered for the 7.62×39mm cartridge, offering powerful stopping power suitable for various combat situations. It is lightweight, durable, and effective in extreme conditions, making it ideal for Indian Army soldiers. The AK-203 is being produced under the Make in India initiative at a facility in Amethi, Uttar Pradesh. Once fully deployed, it will replace the older INSAS rifles, boosting the Army's firepower and combat readiness.

Light Machine Gun

The Light Machine Gun (LMG) is a vital support weapon used by the Indian Army to provide sustained suppressive fire during combat. Traditionally, the Army relied on the INSAS LMG, which offered moderate firepower but faced issues with reliability and performance. To modernize its firepower, the Army has begun inducting Negev NG7 LMGs from Israel, known for their higher rate of fire, better accuracy, and rugged design. LMGs are typically operated by a two-man team and are essential for laying down cover fire during advances or defenses. They are used in a variety of terrains, including counter-insurgency operations. The new generation of LMGs significantly boosts infantry unit effectiveness and fire superiority.

Dragunov Sniper Rifle

The Dragunov Sniper Rifle (SVD) is a semi-automatic designated marksman rifle of Russian origin, widely used by the Indian Army. Chambered for the 7.62×54mmR cartridge, it is designed for accurate long-range shooting, typically up to 800 meters. It features a PSO-1 optical sight, wooden or polymer furniture, and a lightweight design for mobility in the field. The Dragunov is not a true sniper rifle but bridges the gap between standard infantry rifles and specialized sniper weapons. It has served reliably in various terrains, from mountains to deserts. The Indian Army is gradually modernizing its sniper arsenal, but the Dragunov remains in use due to its proven effectiveness.
