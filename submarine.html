Submarine Overview

INS Kalvari

INS Kalvari is a Scorpene-class submarine built in India under Project 75. It's equipped with advanced stealth features and is capable of powerful underwater and surface attacks using torpedoes and missiles.

It enhances India's naval deterrence and underwater warfare capabilities, contributing to maritime security in the Indian Ocean region.

INS Arihant

Arihant was launched on 26 July 2009, the anniversary of Vijay Diwas (Kargil War Victory Day) by Prime Minister Manmohan Singh. After fitting out and extensive sea trials, on 23 February 2016, she was confirmed as ready for operations commissioned in August 2016, and deployed operationally in 2018.

Admiral Verma told reporters on 7 August 2012 that sea trials of Arihant were commencing in coming months, and she was steadily progressing towards operationalisation.[36] On 27 January 2013, Sagarika, the primary armament of Arihant, completed its final developmental test and was later integrated with the submarine.[37] On 10 August 2013, the nuclear reactor of the submarine went critical.
