missiles

The Indian Army possesses a diverse range of missiles that enhance its tactical and strategic capabilities. Key among them are the **BrahMos** cruise missile and **Agni series** ballistic missiles, including the long-range **Agni-V**. For short-range battlefield roles, the **Prithvi-II** missile provides quick-response strike power. **NAG** serves as an advanced anti-tank guided missile, while **Akash** offers strong air defense against aerial threats. These missiles, many of which are indigenously developed, significantly strengthen India's defense posture and deterrence capability.

BrahMos

The **BrahMos missile** is one of the world’s fastest and most advanced supersonic cruise missiles, developed jointly by India and Russia. It can be launched from land, air, sea, and sub-sea platforms, making it extremely versatile and strategic. With a top speed of Mach 2.8 to 3.0 and a range of up to 500 km, BrahMos is capable of delivering precision strikes against high-value targets. Its ability to fly at low altitudes and perform evasive maneuvers makes it difficult to intercept. The missile carries a conventional warhead of up to 300 kg and is guided by an advanced navigation system. BrahMos is a major force multiplier for the Indian armed forces, enhancing their deep-strike capability. Its induction has significantly boosted India’s deterrence and rapid-response capabilities. The missile continues to evolve, with newer variants like BrahMos NG and air-launched versions expanding its operational scope.

Agni-V

**Agni-V** is India’s most powerful intercontinental ballistic missile (ICBM), developed by the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO). It has a range of over 5,000 kilometers, enabling it to reach targets across Asia, Europe, and parts of Africa. Agni-V is capable of carrying a nuclear warhead and uses a three-stage solid-fuel propulsion system for improved mobility and reliability. The missile is canisterized, allowing for quick launch and better storage and transport. It travels at hypersonic speeds during re-entry, making interception extremely difficult. Agni-V plays a crucial role in India’s nuclear deterrence strategy under its “No First Use” policy. Its development showcases India’s advanced missile technology and strategic capabilities. With successful tests and deployment, Agni-V strengthens India’s position as a responsible global nuclear power.

Prithvi-II

**Prithvi-II** is a short-range ballistic missile developed by India under the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme (IGMDP). It has a range of approximately 350 kilometers and can carry both conventional and nuclear warheads up to 1,000 kg. The missile uses liquid fuel propulsion and is known for its high accuracy, thanks to an advanced inertial guidance system. Prithvi-II is primarily operated by the Indian Army and serves as a tactical missile for battlefield use. Its quick deployment and launch capability make it suitable for rapid response scenarios. The missile can hit targets with precision, making it effective against enemy installations and troop concentrations. Regular successful test firings demonstrate its operational readiness and reliability. Prithvi-II continues to be a key component of India's short-range strike capabilities.
